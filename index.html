Daily feeding routine

Refresh your starter with this ratio. Mark each day complete as you go.

0/7
  • Day 1 — Mix it up. Combine 50g flour + 50g water in a clean jar. Stir well, cover loosely, leave at room temp.
    🌾 50g flour · 💧 50g water
  • Day 2 — Look for bubbles. You may see a few small bubbles. Stir and feed: 50g starter + 50g flour + 50g water.
    🫧 Don't worry if it's quiet — it's waking up
  • Day 3 — A sour smell is normal. Discard half, then feed 1:1:1 (50g starter + 50g flour + 50g water).
    👃 It might smell a little funky — that's the bacteria doing their thing
  • Day 4 — Activity picks up. More bubbles, sweeter smell. Continue 1:1:1 feedings every 12 hours.
    📈 Watch for the rise!
  • Day 5 — Testing for readiness. Does it double in 4–8 hours? Sweet & yeasty smell? Switch to 1:2:2 feedings.
    ⏱️ Time how long it takes to double
  • Day 6 — Confirmation round. Feed again, watch for a consistent rise. If it doubles reliably, you're almost there!
    🔁 Three consistent feedings = ready
  • Day 7 — Bake day ready! Your starter should double in 4–6 hours and have a domed, bubbly top.
    🎉 Time to bake something beautiful

How to know your starter is working

Forget the float test — these signs are far more reliable.

📈
Doubles predictably
Triples or doubles within 4–8 hours at room temp, twice in a row.
⛺️
Domed top
Rounded, slightly domed surface — then it relaxes to flat at peak.
🫧
Bubbles everywhere
Bubbles on top, sides, and bottom — not just surface fizz.
👃
Pleasant smell
Yeasty, slightly tangy, like yogurt or ripe fruit. (Acetone = hungry, feed it!)
🥞
Holds its shape
When stirred, slightly stringy and resistant — gluten is alive.
✅
3 good feedings in a row
Consistency is the real test. One lucky rise doesn't count.

Folding techniques

Three techniques, each with its moment. Most bakers use a combination.

Early bulk · Beginner-friendly

Stretch & Fold

  1. Wet your hand (no sticking!)
  2. Reach under one side, stretch up gently
  3. Fold it over to the opposite side
  4. Rotate bowl 90° and repeat 4× total
💡 Best in the first 1–2 hours. Builds strength fast. Skip if the dough tears — let it rest 15 min first.
Mid-to-late bulk · Gentle

Coil Fold

  1. Slide both hands under the center, palms up
  2. Lift straight up — dough hangs like a curtain
  3. Lower it, letting one end tuck under
  4. Rotate 90° and repeat 4× total
💡 Perfect when the dough is gassy and delicate. Creates beautiful surface tension.
Once, very early · Advanced

Lamination

  1. Spritz work surface with water (no flour)
  2. Turn dough out, stretch into a thin sheet
  3. Sprinkle inclusions now (seeds, nuts, fruit)
  4. Fold like a letter: thirds, then thirds again
💡 Maximum gluten in one move. Great for adding seeds. Do it before the dough gets too gassy.

      Troubleshooting

      🍞 Flat loaf, no oven spring

      Likely overproofed, or Dutch oven wasn't hot enough. Check your cold proof time, and preheat the pot for a full 45 minutes.

      🌋 Score blew out the side

      Surface tension was too tight, or score was too shallow. Cut at 30–45° angle, ½ inch deep. Try rice flour dusting to reduce drag.

      🥖 Pale crust

      Your oven runs cooler than the dial (most do!). Use an oven thermometer, or extend the uncovered phase by 5 minutes.

      🔥 Burnt bottom

      Move the rack up one position, or place a baking sheet below the Dutch oven to diffuse the heat.

      😶 Gummy interior

      Underbaked, or cut too early. Internal temp should hit 205–210°F. Then wait at least 1 hour before slicing — the crumb sets as it cools.
